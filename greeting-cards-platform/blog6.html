What to Write in a Wedding Card

Weddings are joyous occasions filled with love, family, and dancing. When you sit down to write a card for the happy couple, you might find yourself staring at a blank page, wondering how to express your happiness for them.

Whether you're writing to a sibling, a coworker, or your oldest friend, the key is to strike a balance between formal congratulations and genuine warmth.

Best Messages to Include

  • "Wishing you a lifetime of love and happiness together. Congratulations on your wedding!"
  • "May the love you share today grow stronger as you grow old together."
  • "So happy to celebrate this beautiful day with you both. Here's to a long and happy marriage!"

Tips to Write Wishes

If you're close to the couple, feel free to use a warmer, more familiar tone. You can even include a lighthearted joke about marriage. If you're a distant relative or an acquaintance, sticking to formal, traditional well-wishes is the best approach.

Always address the card to both members of the couple, even if you only know one of them well. It's their shared day!
